--- OVERVIEW ---
ICD–11 is the international standard for systematic recording, reporting, analysis, interpretation and comparison of mortality and morbidity data. This 11 th revision is the result of an unprecedented collaboration with clinicians, statisticians, classification and IT experts from around the world, making it useable by these groups, as well as by coders.

ICD–11 allows countries to count and identify their most pressing health issues by using an up-to-date and clinically relevant classification system. Health conditions and accidents are assigned ICD–11 codes, resulting in data that can be used by governments to design effective public health policies, and measure their impact, or used for clinical recording.

For the first time, ICD is fully electronic, currently providing access to 17 000 diagnostic categories, with over 100 000 medical diagnostic index terms. The index-based search algorithm interprets more than 1.6 million terms. ICD–11 is easy to install and use online or offline, using free 'container' software.

--- FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS ---
Q: What is ICD-11 and why is it important for patients?
A: ICD-11 (International Classification of Diseases, 11th Revision) is WHO's global standard for classifying diseases, health conditions, and causes of death. It matters for patients because it determines how conditions are coded for insurance claims, hospital records, and public health statistics in India.

Q: Did ICD-11 change the classification of any major conditions?
A: Yes. ICD-11 reclassified gender incongruence from a mental disorder to a condition of sexual health. Gaming disorder was added as a new condition. Burnout was formally defined as an occupational phenomenon. These changes reduce stigma and improve health system responses.

Q: When did India adopt ICD-11?
A: India has been transitioning to ICD-11 implementation from January 2022 (WHO's global implementation date). Healthcare providers and insurance companies in India are adopting the new coding system progressively.

Q: How does ICD-11 affect health insurance claims in India?
A: Insurance companies use ICD codes to process claims. ICD-11 introduces more precise codes, which may affect claim processing. Patients should ensure their diagnosis documentation uses correct ICD codes. Contact your insurer if you face claim issues related to coding.
