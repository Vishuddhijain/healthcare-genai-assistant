--- SECTION 1: ANXIETY DISORDERS ---

--- OVERVIEW ---
Anxiety disorders are the most common mental health conditions worldwide. They involve excessive fear and worry that significantly interfere with daily life. Unlike normal anxiety (e.g., nervousness before an exam), anxiety disorders are persistent, excessive, and disproportionate to the situation.

--- KEY FACTS ---
- Anxiety disorders affect 301 million people globally — the most common mental health condition.
- In India, anxiety disorders affect approximately 3.8% of the population.
- Anxiety disorders are highly treatable, but the majority of people affected receive no treatment.
- Anxiety disorders are more common in women than men.

--- TYPES OF ANXIETY DISORDERS ---
1. Generalised Anxiety Disorder (GAD): Excessive worry about many aspects of life (work, health, family) for 6+ months, difficult to control.
2. Panic Disorder: Recurring unexpected panic attacks — sudden intense fear with physical symptoms (racing heart, sweating, shortness of breath, dizziness). Can feel like a heart attack.
3. Social Anxiety Disorder: Intense fear of social situations due to fear of embarrassment or judgment. Avoidance of social activities.
4. Specific Phobias: Intense fear of specific things (heights, needles, spiders, flying). The fear is out of proportion to actual danger.
5. Separation Anxiety Disorder: Excessive fear of separation from attachment figures (common in children).
6. Agoraphobia: Fear of open spaces or situations where escape may be difficult.

--- SYMPTOMS ---
- Excessive, uncontrollable worry
- Restlessness or feeling on edge
- Fatigue
- Difficulty concentrating
- Irritability
- Muscle tension
- Sleep disturbance
- Physical symptoms: palpitations, sweating, trembling, shortness of breath, chest tightness, nausea, dizziness

--- CAUSES AND RISK FACTORS ---
- Genetics (family history of anxiety)
- Stressful life events (trauma, abuse, loss, major change)
- Certain medical conditions (thyroid disease, heart disease)
- Substance use (caffeine, alcohol can worsen anxiety)
- Personality traits (tendency to be negative or perfectionistic)
- Academic and professional pressure (significant in Indian context)

--- TREATMENT AND MANAGEMENT ---
Anxiety disorders are very treatable:
- Psychological therapies: Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) is highly effective
- Medications: Prescribed by a psychiatrist — do not self-medicate
- Lifestyle: Regular exercise, sleep hygiene, limiting caffeine and alcohol, meditation, yoga
- Talking to a trusted person and not isolating

Resources in India:
- iCall helpline: 9152987821 (TISS — free counselling)
- Tele MANAS: 14416 (24/7 free mental health support)
- Government psychiatric outpatient departments at district hospitals (free)

--- SECTION 2: AUTISM SPECTRUM DISORDER (ASD) ---

--- OVERVIEW ---
Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a neurodevelopmental condition characterised by differences in social communication, behaviour, and sensory processing. ASD is not a disease — it is a different way of experiencing the world. People with ASD have diverse strengths and challenges. With the right support, people with ASD can lead fulfilling lives.

--- KEY FACTS ---
- ASD affects approximately 1 in 100 children globally (WHO estimate).
- In India, ASD prevalence is estimated at 1–1.5% (over 1 crore individuals).
- ASD is more commonly diagnosed in boys than girls (4:1 ratio).
- ASD is a spectrum — it affects people very differently. Some are highly capable; some need significant daily support.
- ASD is not caused by vaccines — this has been conclusively disproven.
- Early intervention (before age 5) dramatically improves outcomes.

--- SIGNS AND SYMPTOMS ---
Early signs in children (2–3 years — consult a doctor if you notice):
- Not responding to their name by 12 months
- Not pointing at objects to show interest by 14 months
- Not playing pretend games by 18 months
- Avoiding eye contact
- Losing language skills they previously had
- Repeating words or phrases over and over (echolalia)
- Unusual sensitivity to sounds, lights, textures, or tastes
- Strong insistence on routines; distress at small changes
- Very focused interest in specific topics

In older children and adults:
- Difficulty understanding others' emotions or perspectives
- Challenges with back-and-forth conversation
- Difficulty making or keeping friends
- Preference for routines
- Repetitive movements (hand-flapping, rocking)
- Exceptional skills in specific areas (memory, mathematics, music) in some individuals

--- CAUSES ---
The exact causes are not fully understood. Contributing factors include:
- Genetic factors (most significant)
- Advanced parental age
- Certain prenatal environmental exposures
- NOT caused by vaccines, bad parenting, or diet

--- SUPPORT AND INTERVENTION ---
- Early diagnosis and therapy are key — the earlier, the better
- Applied Behaviour Analysis (ABA) therapy
- Speech and language therapy
- Occupational therapy
- Inclusive education programmes
- Family counselling and support

India resources:
- NIEPMD (National Institute for Empowerment of Persons with Multiple Disabilities): niepmd.tn.nic.in
- National Trust: nationaltrust.gov.in — provides legal support, welfare schemes for autism
- RPwD Act 2016: People with ASD have rights to education, employment support, and non-discrimination in India

--- FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS ---
Q: Does anxiety go away on its own?
A: Mild situational anxiety often resolves when the stressor passes. But anxiety disorders that interfere with daily life generally need treatment. Seeking help early is better — conditions can worsen without support.

Q: Are anxiety and depression the same?
A: No. Anxiety is primarily about excessive fear and worry. Depression is primarily about persistent low mood and loss of interest. However, they frequently occur together. Both are treatable.

Q: Is autism a mental illness?
A: No. Autism is a neurodevelopmental condition — a different way the brain develops and processes information. It is not a mental illness or intellectual disability (though some individuals with ASD may also have intellectual disability).

Q: Can adults be diagnosed with autism?
A: Yes. Many people, especially women and those with milder presentations, are diagnosed in adulthood. It is never too late to seek diagnosis and support.

--- IMPORTANT SAFETY NOTE ---
Anxiety disorders and ASD require professional assessment and support. Contact a psychiatrist, psychologist, or child development specialist. For crisis support, call Tele MANAS: 14416.
