--- OVERVIEW ---
Good nutrition is a foundation of good health. A healthy diet helps protect against malnutrition, non-communicable diseases like diabetes, heart disease, stroke, and certain cancers. Unhealthy diet and lack of physical activity are leading global risks to health.

--- KEY PRINCIPLES OF A HEALTHY DIET ---

1. EAT PLENTY OF FRUITS AND VEGETABLES
- Aim for at least 5 portions (400g total) of fruits and vegetables per day
- Reduces risk of heart disease, stroke, and certain cancers
- Include a variety — different colours provide different nutrients
- Choose fresh, seasonal, local produce when possible

2. REDUCE SALT INTAKE
- Limit salt to less than 5 grams (about 1 teaspoon) per day
- Excess salt is a major cause of hypertension and stroke
- Avoid adding extra salt to cooked food
- Read food labels — many packaged foods have high hidden salt

3. LIMIT SUGAR
- Reduce free sugars to less than 10% of total energy intake (ideally less than 5%)
- Avoid sugary drinks (sodas, packaged juices, energy drinks)
- Avoid processed sweets, cakes, and biscuits
- Excess sugar leads to weight gain, dental problems, and diabetes

4. REDUCE UNHEALTHY FATS
- Avoid trans fats (found in fried/processed foods, margarine, bakery products)
- Limit saturated fats (found in red meat, butter, ghee, cream, cheese)
- Use unsaturated fats (found in fish, avocado, nuts, olive oil, sunflower oil)
- Trans fats increase risk of heart disease; replace with unsaturated fats

5. EAT ENOUGH PROTEIN
- Include pulses (dal, rajma, chana), legumes, nuts, seeds
- Eggs, fish, and lean poultry are good sources
- Plant-based proteins are healthier and more sustainable

6. STAY HYDRATED
- Drink 8–10 glasses (2–2.5 litres) of water daily
- Water is the best drink — avoid sugary or carbonated drinks
- Increase intake in hot weather or during physical activity

--- PHYSICAL ACTIVITY RECOMMENDATIONS ---
WHO recommends for adults (18–64 years):
- At least 150–300 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week (e.g., brisk walking, cycling)
- OR at least 75–150 minutes of vigorous-intensity activity per week (e.g., running, sports)
- Muscle-strengthening activities (exercises involving major muscle groups) at least 2 days per week

For children (5–17 years):
- At least 60 minutes of moderate to vigorous physical activity per day
- Limit sedentary time (screen time, sitting)

Benefits of regular physical activity:
- Reduces risk of heart disease, Type 2 diabetes, and several cancers
- Helps maintain healthy weight
- Improves mental health and mood
- Strengthens bones and muscles
- Improves sleep quality

--- COMMON NUTRITIONAL DEFICIENCIES IN INDIA ---
1. Iron deficiency anemia: Very common, especially in women and children. Eat iron-rich foods: green leafy vegetables, pulses, dates, jaggery, meat. Vitamin C (lemon, amla) helps absorb iron.
2. Vitamin D deficiency: Common in India despite sunshine. Get 15–20 minutes of morning sunlight daily. Food sources: eggs, fatty fish, fortified milk.
3. Iodine deficiency: Prevented by using iodised salt.
4. Vitamin B12 deficiency: Common in vegetarians. Sources: dairy, eggs. Supplements if needed (consult doctor).
5. Calcium deficiency: Important for bones. Sources: milk, curd, paneer, ragi, sesame seeds.

--- PREVENTIVE HEALTH SCREENINGS (INDIA) ---
Under Ayushman Bharat Health & Wellness Centres, free screenings include:
- Blood pressure check
- Blood sugar test (diabetes screening)
- BMI / obesity screening
- Cancer screening (oral, cervical, breast) for eligible age groups
Find your nearest HWC: nhp.gov.in or call Ayushman Bharat helpline: 14555

--- HEALTHY WEIGHT ---
Body Mass Index (BMI) is used to assess weight status:
- Below 18.5: Underweight
- 18.5–24.9: Normal (healthy)
- 25–29.9: Overweight
- 30 and above: Obese

For Indian adults, a BMI above 23 increases risk of lifestyle diseases.

--- WHEN TO CONSULT A NUTRITIONIST OR DOCTOR ---
- If you have diabetes, hypertension, or kidney disease — diet changes must be doctor-supervised
- For significant unexplained weight loss or gain
- If you suspect a nutritional deficiency
- For children's nutritional concerns

--- IMPORTANT SAFETY NOTE ---
General dietary advice is provided for awareness only. Specific diet plans for chronic conditions must be prescribed by a qualified nutritionist or doctor.
