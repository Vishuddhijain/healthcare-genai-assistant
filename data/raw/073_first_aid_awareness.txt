--- OVERVIEW ---
First aid is the immediate assistance given to a person who has been injured or suddenly taken ill, before the arrival of professional medical help. Knowing basic first aid can save lives and prevent conditions from getting worse. Always call emergency services (112) for serious situations.

--- GENERAL FIRST AID PRINCIPLES (DR ABC) ---
D - Danger: Check for safety. Ensure the area is safe for you and the victim.
R - Response: Check if the person is conscious. Call out, tap shoulders gently.
A - Airway: Ensure the airway is open (tilt head back, lift chin).
B - Breathing: Check if the person is breathing normally.
C - Circulation/CPR: If not breathing, begin CPR if trained.

ALWAYS call 112 in an emergency.

--- COMMON FIRST AID SITUATIONS ---

1. CHOKING
Signs: Difficulty breathing, coughing, unable to speak, clutching throat.
For conscious adult/child (over 1 year):
- Give up to 5 firm back blows between shoulder blades
- If not resolved, give up to 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich manoeuvre)
- Alternate between back blows and abdominal thrusts until object dislodged or person loses consciousness
For infants: Use back blows and chest thrusts (do not do abdominal thrusts)
Call 112 if choking does not resolve immediately.

2. BURNS
Minor burns (redness, pain, no blisters on small area):
- Cool with running cold water for at least 10 minutes
- Do NOT use ice, butter, toothpaste, or oil — they worsen burns
- Cover loosely with clean non-fluffy material
- Take to a doctor if needed

Seek emergency care for:
- Large burns, or burns involving the face, hands, genitals, or joints
- Burns with blisters over a large area
- Chemical or electrical burns
- Burns in children or elderly

3. BLEEDING
- Apply firm, direct pressure with a clean cloth or bandage
- Do not remove the cloth if it gets soaked — add more on top
- Elevate the injured part above heart level if possible
- Maintain pressure for at least 10 minutes
- For severe or spurting bleeding: Call 112 immediately

4. FRACTURES / BROKEN BONES
- Do not move the person unless in immediate danger
- Immobilise the injured area — support it in the position found
- Do not try to straighten the limb
- Apply ice pack (wrapped in cloth) to reduce swelling
- Seek emergency medical care

5. FAINTING
- Lay the person on their back
- Raise their legs 15–30 cm (to improve blood flow to brain)
- Loosen tight clothing (collar, belt)
- If they do not regain consciousness in 1–2 minutes, call 112
- Once conscious, do not let them stand up immediately

6. SNAKE BITE
- Keep the person calm and still (movement increases venom spread)
- Immobilise the bitten limb below the level of the heart
- Remove watches, rings, or tight clothing near the bite
- Do NOT cut the wound, suck out venom, or apply tourniquet
- Rush to nearest hospital IMMEDIATELY — antivenom is available free at government hospitals

7. HEAT STROKE
Signs: Very high body temperature, hot and dry skin, confusion, loss of consciousness.
- Move person to shade or cool area immediately
- Cool the person — apply cool water to skin, fan them, use wet cloth on neck, armpits, groin
- Give cool water to drink if conscious and able to swallow
- Call 112 — heat stroke is a medical emergency

8. SUSPECTED HEART ATTACK
Warning signs: Chest pain/pressure, pain radiating to arm/jaw, sweating, shortness of breath, nausea.
- Call 112 immediately
- Have the person sit or lie in a comfortable position (not flat)
- Loosen tight clothing
- If trained and advised, assist with any prescribed medication the person may have
- Be prepared to perform CPR if the person loses consciousness and stops breathing

9. CHOKING IN INFANTS (Under 1 year)
- Hold infant face-down on your forearm
- Give 5 back blows with heel of hand between shoulder blades
- Turn infant face-up, give 5 chest thrusts (2 fingers on lower breast bone)
- Alternate until object is cleared or infant loses consciousness
- Call 112 immediately

--- WHEN TO CALL 112 ---
Always call 112 for:
- Chest pain or suspected heart attack/stroke
- Difficulty breathing
- Serious burns, deep cuts with heavy bleeding
- Broken bones with deformity or severe pain
- Loss of consciousness
- Seizures (convulsions)
- Suspected poisoning
- Severe allergic reactions

--- IMPORTANT SAFETY NOTE ---
First aid information is for awareness and initial response only. It is NOT a substitute for professional medical care. Always seek medical attention after administering first aid, and call 112 for emergencies.
