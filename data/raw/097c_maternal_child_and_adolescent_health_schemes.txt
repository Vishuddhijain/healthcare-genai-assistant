--- OVERVIEW ---
The Government of India runs a coordinated set of schemes covering pregnancy, childbirth, infancy, childhood, and adolescence — aiming to reduce maternal mortality, infant mortality, and malnutrition, and to ensure every mother and child receives free, quality care. These schemes are implemented mainly through the National Health Mission (NHM), Anganwadi centres (ICDS), and ASHA workers at the community level. For health insurance schemes, see 097b; for immunisation and disease control, see 097c.

--- 1. JANANI SURAKSHA YOJANA (JSY) ---
What it is: A safe motherhood cash assistance scheme under NHM to promote institutional delivery (delivery in a health facility rather than at home) and reduce maternal and neonatal mortality.
Who it benefits: Pregnant women, especially from BPL (Below Poverty Line) families and in Low Performing States (LPS) with low institutional delivery rates; in High Performing States, BPL/SC/ST women are covered.
Benefits:
- Cash assistance for institutional delivery — Rural areas: ₹1,400 (LPS) for the mother; Urban areas: ₹1,000 (LPS); amounts differ for High Performing States.
- Free antenatal check-ups, free delivery (including caesarean section where required), and free postnatal care at government facilities.
- ASHA workers receive an incentive for accompanying and facilitating institutional delivery.
How to access: Register pregnancy with the local ASHA worker, ANM, or at the nearest Anganwadi/PHC as early as possible; cash assistance is provided after institutional delivery, usually via direct bank transfer.

--- 2. JANANI SHISHU SURAKSHA KARYAKRAM (JSSK) ---
What it is: An entitlement-based scheme guaranteeing absolutely free and cashless services for pregnant women and sick newborns at government health facilities.
Coverage for pregnant women: Free delivery including caesarean section, free drugs and consumables, free diagnostics (blood, urine tests, ultrasound where available), free diet during stay in hospital, free blood transfusion if required, free transport from home to health facility, between facilities in case of referral, and drop back home after delivery.
Coverage for sick newborns (up to 30 days after birth): Free treatment, free drugs and consumables, free diagnostics, free blood transfusion, free transport, and exemption from all user charges.
How to access: Available automatically to all pregnant women and sick newborns delivering/being treated at public health institutions — no separate registration required, but ensure the facility staff are informed this is a JSSK entitlement if any charges are requested.

--- 3. PRADHAN MANTRI MATRU VANDANA YOJANA (PMMVY) ---
What it is: A maternity benefit programme providing cash incentives to pregnant and lactating women to compensate for wage loss and encourage adequate nutrition and care during pregnancy and after delivery.
Benefit: ₹5,000 (in three instalments, linked to specific conditions such as early pregnancy registration, antenatal check-up, and child birth registration plus first cycle of vaccination) is provided for the first living child; an additional benefit is provided for a second child if it is a girl child, under a related component, to address the declining child sex ratio.
Eligibility: All pregnant women and lactating mothers, excluding those who are in regular government/PSU employment or already receiving similar benefits under other statutory schemes.
How to apply: Through the Anganwadi Worker (AWW) or ASHA worker, or directly via the PMMVY web portal/app, using Aadhaar-linked bank account details for Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT).

--- 4. PRADHAN MANTRI SURAKSHIT MATRITVA ABHIYAN (PMSMA) ---
What it is: A programme providing fixed-day (9th of every month), free, comprehensive antenatal check-ups by a specialist/doctor to all pregnant women, with a special focus on identifying and managing high-risk pregnancies.
Services: Free antenatal check-up including blood pressure, blood sugar, blood tests, ultrasound (where available), and identification of high-risk pregnancy markers (e.g., severe anaemia, pre-eclampsia, gestational diabetes).
Where available: Designated PHCs, CHCs, and district hospitals on the 9th of every month (or next working day if the 9th is a holiday); look for a "PMSMA Diwas" notice at your local health facility.
How to access: No special registration needed — pregnant women can visit on the designated day; ASHA workers help mobilise and accompany women in the community.

--- 5. RASHTRIYA BAL SWASTHYA KARYAKRAM (RBSK) ---
What it is: A child health screening and early intervention programme covering birth defects, diseases, deficiencies, and developmental delays/disabilities (the "4 Ds") in children from birth to 18 years.
Coverage: Screens for 30+ health conditions including congenital heart defects, cleft lip/palate, vision and hearing impairment, developmental delays, neural tube defects, and nutritional deficiencies; provides free management and treatment, including surgery for conditions like congenital heart disease and cleft lip/palate, often linked with PM-JAY for high-cost procedures.
How it works: Dedicated Mobile Health Teams visit Anganwadi centres and schools periodically to screen children; children needing further evaluation/treatment are referred to District Early Intervention Centres (DEICs) or higher facilities, with free follow-up.
How to access: Ensure your child attends Anganwadi/school screening camps; ask your Anganwadi worker or school about RBSK screening schedules.

--- 6. INTEGRATED CHILD DEVELOPMENT SERVICES (ICDS) / ANGANWADI SERVICES ---
What it is: India's flagship programme for early childhood care and development, delivered through a network of Anganwadi Centres (AWCs) across the country.
Services provided free at Anganwadi Centres:
- Supplementary nutrition for children under 6, pregnant women, and lactating mothers
- Growth monitoring (height/weight tracking) for children under 6
- Pre-school non-formal education for children aged 3-6
- Health check-ups, referral services, and immunisation support in coordination with ANMs
- Nutrition and health education for mothers
How to access: Locate your nearest Anganwadi Centre (usually within walking distance in most villages and urban slums) — registration is done by the Anganwadi Worker (AWW) for eligible children, pregnant women, and lactating mothers in the catchment area.

--- 7. POSHAN ABHIYAAN (NATIONAL NUTRITION MISSION) ---
What it is: An overarching mission to improve nutritional outcomes for children, adolescent girls, pregnant women, and lactating mothers by reducing stunting, underweight, anaemia, and low birth weight through convergence of multiple schemes (ICDS, health programmes, and Jal Jeevan Mission for clean water).
Components: Monthly growth monitoring at Anganwadis using digital tools (Poshan Tracker), community-based events (Poshan Pakhwada/Poshan Maah), micronutrient supplementation (Vitamin A, Iron-Folic Acid), and convergence with Anaemia Mukt Bharat.
How to access: Available automatically through Anganwadi services (see ICDS above) and government health facilities; growth monitoring data of children is recorded digitally to track progress.

--- 8. RASHTRIYA KISHOR SWASTHYA KARYAKRAM (RKSK) ---
What it is: India's adolescent health programme (covering ages 10-19), addressing nutrition, mental health, injuries and violence (including gender-based violence), non-communicable diseases, substance misuse, and sexual and reproductive health.
Services: Adolescent-friendly health clinics ("Saathiya" centres/AFHCs) at PHCs and CHCs offering confidential counselling and services; peer educators ("Saathiya") trained to provide information and referrals on adolescent health topics.
Helpline/Resource: Saathiya SAArthi mobile app and helpline provide adolescent health information in a confidential, youth-friendly manner.
How to access: Visit the Adolescent Friendly Health Clinic at your nearest PHC/CHC, or speak with a trained peer educator/Saathiya in your school or community where available.

--- 9. NATIONAL DEWORMING DAY (NDD) ---
What it is: A biannual (twice yearly, typically February and August) nationwide programme to administer a single dose of deworming medicine (albendazole) to all children and adolescents aged 1-19 years, free of cost.
Where: Government and government-aided schools, and through Anganwadi centres for pre-school children (1-5 years) not enrolled in school.
How to access: No registration needed — medicine is administered on designated NDD days at school/Anganwadi; parents can ask the school or Anganwadi worker about the next scheduled date.

--- TREATMENT (AWARENESS ONLY) ---
- Treatment details are for awareness only. Consult a qualified healthcare professional. These schemes provide access to and financing of care but do not themselves constitute medical advice.

--- WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR ---
- Pregnant women experiencing warning signs such as heavy bleeding, severe headache, blurred vision, severe abdominal pain, reduced fetal movements, or high fever should go to the nearest government health facility immediately — transport and treatment are free under JSSK. For sick newborns showing difficulty breathing, refusal to feed, lethargy, fever, or convulsions, seek immediate care at a government facility — treatment is free under JSSK. Use emergency number 112 for life-threatening situations.

--- INDIA-SPECIFIC CONTEXT ---
- Benefit amounts (e.g., JSY cash assistance, PMMVY instalments) and the specific list of conditions screened under RBSK are periodically revised. Always verify current details with your ASHA worker, Anganwadi worker, nearest PHC, or the NHM/WCD ministry websites.

--- FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS ---
Q: Is delivery in a government hospital really completely free, including for caesarean section?
A: Yes. Under Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK), pregnant women delivering at public health facilities are entitled to free delivery (including caesarean section), free drugs, free diagnostics, free diet, free blood transfusion if needed, and free transport — with no user charges. If you are asked to pay, inform the facility staff that this is a JSSK entitlement and you can also raise it with the Health Helpline 104.

Q: How and when do I apply for the ₹5,000 PMMVY maternity benefit?
A: Register your pregnancy as early as possible with your Anganwadi Worker or ASHA, who will help you apply through the PMMVY portal/app using your Aadhaar-linked bank account. The benefit is paid in instalments linked to early registration, completing antenatal check-ups, and registering the child's birth and first vaccinations.

Q: My child was born with a heart defect/cleft lip. Is treatment available for free?
A: Yes, potentially. The Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK) screens children for birth defects including congenital heart disease and cleft lip/palate and refers them for free treatment, including surgery, often in coordination with Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY for high-cost procedures. Ask your Anganwadi worker about RBSK screening or visit the District Early Intervention Centre (DEIC) at your district hospital.

Q: What is the "9th of every month" antenatal check-up I keep hearing about?
A: This refers to Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA), under which government health facilities provide a free, specialist-led antenatal check-up to all pregnant women on the 9th of every month (or the next working day), with special attention to identifying high-risk pregnancies. No prior appointment is typically needed.

Q: Are Anganwadi services only for very poor families, or can any family use them?
A: Anganwadi Centres under ICDS are primarily aimed at children under 6, pregnant women, and lactating mothers, particularly in underserved communities, but services such as growth monitoring, immunisation support, and pre-school education are broadly accessible at the community level. Contact your local Anganwadi Worker to understand services available in your area.
