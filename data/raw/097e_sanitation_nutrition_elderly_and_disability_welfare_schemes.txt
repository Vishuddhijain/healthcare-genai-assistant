--- OVERVIEW ---
Beyond direct medical care and insurance, the Government of India runs several large-scale schemes that address the underlying determinants of health — clean water, sanitation, clean cooking fuel — as well as dedicated programmes for the health and welfare of senior citizens and persons with disabilities. These schemes have a major preventive impact on disease burden (e.g., reducing diarrhoeal disease, respiratory illness, and indoor air pollution-related conditions). For health insurance schemes, see 097b; for maternal, child, and adolescent schemes, see 097c; for disease control and immunisation, see 097d.

--- 1. SWACHH BHARAT MISSION (SBM) ---
What it is: A national campaign to improve sanitation and eliminate open defecation through construction of household and community toilets, and to improve solid and liquid waste management.
Health relevance: Open defecation and poor sanitation are major drivers of diarrhoeal diseases, soil-transmitted helminth (worm) infections, and child malnutrition/stunting. SBM directly supports prevention of these conditions.
Benefits: Financial assistance for construction of individual household toilets for eligible households (particularly in rural areas, where assistance is provided through SBM-Gramin); community sanitary complexes in areas where individual toilets are not feasible.
How to access: Apply through the Gram Panchayat (rural) or Urban Local Body (urban) for toilet construction assistance; check eligibility criteria with local administration, as some categories of households (e.g., those already having access to toilets) may not be eligible for incentive amounts.

--- 2. JAL JEEVAN MISSION (JJM) ---
What it is: A mission to provide safe and adequate drinking water through individual household tap connections to all rural households ("Har Ghar Jal").
Health relevance: Access to safe piped drinking water reduces the burden of waterborne diseases such as diarrhoea, typhoid, hepatitis A, and cholera, and reduces the time burden (especially on women and girls) of fetching water from distant sources.
How to access: Implementation is at the village/Gram Panchayat level through the Village Water and Sanitation Committee/Paani Samiti; households can check with their Gram Panchayat on the status of tap water connections in their village under JJM.

--- 3. PRADHAN MANTRI UJJWALA YOJANA (PMUY) ---
What it is: A scheme providing free LPG (cooking gas) connections to women from below poverty line (BPL) and other eligible households, to replace traditional cooking fuels (wood, coal, dung cakes, kerosene).
Health relevance: Household (indoor) air pollution from solid fuel cooking is a major cause of chronic respiratory disease (COPD), pneumonia in children, eye problems, and adverse pregnancy outcomes, particularly affecting women and young children. PMUY directly reduces this exposure.
Benefits: Free LPG gas connection (cylinder, regulator, and one-time financial support for the connection) in the name of the woman of the household; some states/schemes also provide subsidised refills for eligible beneficiaries.
How to apply: Apply through any LPG distributor (Indane, Bharat Gas, HP Gas) with proof of BPL status/eligibility documents (e.g., ration card under specific categories, Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana beneficiary list, or other government-notified eligibility lists); apply online via pmuy.gov.in or through the distributor.

--- 4. NATIONAL PROGRAMME FOR HEALTH CARE OF THE ELDERLY (NPHCE) ---
What it is: A programme to provide accessible, affordable, and high-quality healthcare services to senior citizens (typically defined as 60 years and above), with a focus on both promotive/preventive and curative/rehabilitative care.
Services: Dedicated geriatric clinics/wards at district hospitals and CHCs in many districts, geriatric-friendly OPD services at PHCs/Ayushman Arogya Mandirs (including separate queues/priority for elderly where implemented), home-based care for bed-ridden elderly through outreach teams in some districts, and training of health workers in geriatric care.
Related scheme: For health insurance specifically for senior citizens (70+), see the Ayushman Vay Vandana Card described in file 097b.
How to access: Ask at your district hospital or CHC about geriatric clinic days/services; home-based care availability varies by district — check with your local health authorities or ASHA worker.

--- 5. RASHTRIYA VAYOSHRI YOJANA (RVY) ---
What it is: A scheme providing free assistive devices and living aids to senior citizens belonging to BPL households, to improve their mobility and quality of life.
Devices typically provided: Walking sticks, elbow crutches, walkers, wheelchairs, hearing aids, spectacles (for low vision/cataract-related issues), and artificial dentures, depending on individual assessment.
Who is eligible: Senior citizens (60+) belonging to BPL families, as identified through camps organised under the scheme.
How to access: RVY is implemented through periodic camps organised at the district level (often via ALIMCO - Artificial Limbs Manufacturing Corporation of India), where eligible seniors are assessed and provided devices free of cost; check with the District Social Welfare Officer for upcoming camps in your area.

--- 6. ASSISTANCE TO DISABLED PERSONS FOR PURCHASE/FITTING OF AIDS AND APPLIANCES (ADIP SCHEME) ---
What it is: A scheme under the Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities providing financial assistance for purchase, fitting, or replacement of durable, sophisticated, and modern standard aids and appliances for persons with disabilities, to promote their physical, social, and psychological rehabilitation.
Devices covered: Wheelchairs, tricycles, hearing aids, artificial limbs/calipers, Braille kits, and other assistive devices appropriate to the type of disability, depending on assessed need and income criteria.
Who is eligible: Indian citizens with disabilities (as certified under the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016) meeting the scheme's income criteria, with priority typically given to those from economically weaker sections.
How to access: Apply through implementing agencies/NGOs empanelled under ADIP, or through camps organised by ALIMCO and state social welfare departments; a disability certificate is generally required, which can be obtained from designated government medical boards/hospitals.

--- 7. NATIONAL TRUST FOR THE WELFARE OF PERSONS WITH AUTISM, CEREBRAL PALSY, MENTAL RETARDATION AND MULTIPLE DISABILITIES ---
What it is: A statutory body under the Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment that runs schemes for persons with the four specified disabilities (autism, cerebral palsy, intellectual disability, and multiple disabilities), including respite care, day care, and guardianship-related support.
Schemes: Includes programmes such as "Disha" (early intervention and school readiness), "Vikaas" (day care), "Samarth" (respite care), and "Gharaunda" (group home for adults), implemented through registered organisations across states.
How to access: Contact a National Trust-registered organisation in your area (list available through the National Trust/state disability welfare departments) for assessment and enrolment in relevant programmes.

--- 8. PRADHAN MANTRI MATSYA SAMPADA / FOOD FORTIFICATION AND EAT RIGHT INDIA (NUTRITION-RELATED, FSSAI) ---
What it is: While not a direct healthcare scheme, the Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) runs the "Eat Right India" movement and supports food fortification (e.g., fortified rice, edible oil, milk with added vitamins) to address widespread micronutrient deficiencies (such as Vitamin A, iron, and iodine deficiency) at a population level.
Health relevance: Fortified staple foods distributed through the Public Distribution System (PDS), ICDS, and Mid-Day Meal/PM-POSHAN schemes help reduce micronutrient deficiencies, particularly anaemia, among children, women, and economically vulnerable populations.
How to access: Fortified rice and other fortified staples are increasingly distributed through ration shops (PDS) and school meal programmes automatically in many states — no separate application needed; check with your local ration shop or school for current fortification status.

--- PREVENTION ---
- The schemes in this file (sanitation, safe water, clean cooking fuel) function as broad preventive/environmental health interventions, reducing the burden of diarrhoeal disease, respiratory illness, and malnutrition. See disease-specific files (e.g., diarrhoeal disease, pneumonia, household air pollution, soil-transmitted helminths) for detailed prevention guidance.

--- ENVIRONMENTAL CONTROL ---
- Swachh Bharat Mission (sanitation/open defecation reduction), Jal Jeevan Mission (safe drinking water access), and PMUY (clean cooking fuel) are themselves major environmental health/control interventions at the household and community level, as described in items 1-3 above.

--- WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR ---
- Seek medical care for severe, persistent, worsening, or red-flag symptoms regardless of scheme eligibility status. For elderly individuals, seek prompt medical attention for falls, sudden confusion, chest pain, or breathing difficulty. Use emergency number 112 for life-threatening situations and Elderline 14567 for elder welfare concerns.

--- INDIA-SPECIFIC CONTEXT ---
- Eligibility criteria, device lists, and benefit amounts for RVY, ADIP, PMUY, and related schemes are periodically revised and may vary by state. Always verify current details with the District Social Welfare Office, your Gram Panchayat/Urban Local Body, or the relevant ministry website (DEPwD, MoHFW, Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas for PMUY).

--- FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS ---
Q: How does getting an LPG connection under Ujjwala Yojana actually improve health?
A: Cooking with solid fuels (wood, dung, coal) on open fires produces high levels of indoor air pollution, which is strongly linked to chronic respiratory disease (such as COPD) in adults — especially women who cook daily — and to pneumonia and other respiratory infections in young children who are frequently nearby. Switching to LPG under PMUY significantly reduces this exposure and the associated disease burden.

Q: My grandparent needs a wheelchair or hearing aid but we cannot afford one. What government help is available?
A: Two main options: (1) The Rashtriya Vayoshri Yojana (RVY) provides free assistive devices (wheelchairs, hearing aids, walking sticks, spectacles, dentures) to BPL senior citizens (60+) through periodic district-level camps. (2) The ADIP scheme provides assistive devices to persons with disabilities of any age (with a valid disability certificate) meeting income criteria. Contact your District Social Welfare Office for both.

Q: Is there a government helpline specifically for elderly people facing health or welfare problems?
A: Yes. Elderline (14567) is the national helpline for senior citizens, providing information, guidance, and assistance on issues including elder abuse, access to schemes like NPHCE (geriatric healthcare) and RVY (assistive devices), and general welfare support.

Q: My family doesn't yet have a toilet at home or a piped water connection. How do I apply for help under government schemes?
A: For toilet construction assistance, contact your Gram Panchayat (in rural areas) or Urban Local Body (in urban areas) regarding the Swachh Bharat Mission. For piped drinking water connections, check with your Gram Panchayat/Village Water and Sanitation Committee about the status of the Jal Jeevan Mission ("Har Ghar Jal") rollout in your village, as implementation is being progressively extended across the country.

Q: My child has autism or cerebral palsy. What support is available beyond medical treatment?
A: The National Trust for the Welfare of Persons with Autism, Cerebral Palsy, Mental Retardation and Multiple Disabilities runs programmes such as "Disha" (early intervention), "Vikaas" (day care), and "Samarth" (respite care) through registered organisations. Contact a National Trust-registered organisation in your area or your state disability welfare department for assessment and enrolment.
