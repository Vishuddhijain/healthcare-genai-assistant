--- OVERVIEW ---
The Government of India runs over 300 health-related schemes and programmes at the central level alone (with many more at the state level), covering health insurance, maternal and child health, disease control, immunisation, primary care infrastructure, nutrition, sanitation, and welfare for senior citizens and persons with disabilities. To make this information easier to use, the schemes have been organised into four detailed companion files by category. This file serves as an index/overview — use it to identify which companion file covers the scheme or topic you are interested in.

--- HOW THE GOVERNMENT SCHEME FILES ARE ORGANISED ---

FILE 097b — Health Insurance and Financial Protection Schemes
Covers: Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY (₹5 lakh family health insurance), Ayushman Vay Vandana Card (additional ₹5 lakh cover for citizens aged 70+), Central Government Health Scheme (CGHS) for government employees/pensioners, Employees' State Insurance Scheme (ESIC) for formal sector workers, Rashtriya Arogya Nidhi (RAN) for financial assistance with major illnesses, and examples of state-level health insurance top-up schemes.
Ask about: "Am I eligible for Ayushman Bharat", "health insurance for senior citizens", "CGHS for government employees", "financial help for cancer/heart surgery if not covered by insurance".

FILE 097c — Maternal, Child, and Adolescent Health Schemes
Covers: Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY) cash assistance for institutional delivery, Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK) for completely free maternal and newborn care, Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY) maternity benefit (₹5,000), Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA) free antenatal check-ups on the 9th of every month, Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK) child health screening, Integrated Child Development Services (ICDS)/Anganwadi services, POSHAN Abhiyaan (nutrition mission), Rashtriya Kishor Swasthya Karyakram (RKSK) adolescent health programme, and National Deworming Day.
Ask about: "free delivery in government hospital", "maternity benefit scheme", "free antenatal checkup", "Anganwadi services", "child health screening for birth defects", "adolescent health clinics".

FILE 097d — Disease Control, Immunisation, and Primary Care Schemes
Covers: Ayushman Bharat Health and Wellness Centres/Ayushman Arogya Mandirs, the Universal Immunisation Programme (UIP) and national immunisation schedule, Mission Indradhanush for catch-up vaccination, National Tuberculosis Elimination Programme (NTEP) including Ni-kshay Poshan Yojana, National Vector Borne Disease Control Programme (NVBDCP) for malaria/dengue/filariasis/kala-azar, National Leprosy Eradication Programme (NLEP), National Programme for Prevention and Control of NCDs (NP-NCD), National Mental Health Programme (NMHP) and Tele MANAS helpline, National Tobacco Control Programme (NTCP), and the Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABHA health ID).
Ask about: "free vaccination schedule for children", "TB testing and treatment", "malaria/dengue treatment at government hospital", "free mental health helpline", "quit smoking support", "NCD screening for diabetes and blood pressure", "what is ABHA health ID".

FILE 097e — Sanitation, Nutrition, Elderly Care, and Disability Welfare Schemes
Covers: Swachh Bharat Mission (sanitation/toilets), Jal Jeevan Mission (piped drinking water), Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (free LPG connections), National Programme for Health Care of the Elderly (NPHCE), Rashtriya Vayoshri Yojana (free assistive devices for BPL seniors), ADIP scheme (assistive devices for persons with disabilities), National Trust schemes for autism/cerebral palsy/intellectual disability, and food fortification under Eat Right India.
Ask about: "free LPG connection scheme", "toilet construction government scheme", "free wheelchair or hearing aid for elderly", "geriatric healthcare services", "disability assistive devices scheme", "helpline for senior citizens".

--- FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS ---
Q: How do I check if I am eligible for Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY?
A: Visit pmjay.gov.in, enter your mobile number or ration card details, or call the PM-JAY helpline at 14555. You can also visit the nearest Ayushman Bharat empanelled hospital, Ayushman Arogya Mandir, or Common Service Centre (CSC) to check eligibility and get your Ayushman card. See file 097b for full details.

Q: What is the difference between PHC, CHC, and district hospital in India?
A: PHC (Primary Health Centre)/Ayushman Arogya Mandir: Serves 30,000-50,000 population; handles basic outpatient care, maternal health, vaccinations, and NCD/TB screening. CHC (Community Health Centre): Serves 80,000-1,20,000; has specialist services (surgery, gynaecology, paediatrics). District Hospital: Full secondary care including emergency surgery and specialised services. See file 097d for related programmes delivered at each level.

Q: Are all medicines really free at government hospitals?
A: Essential medicines on the National List of Essential Medicines (NLEM) should be available free at all government hospitals. If medicines are unavailable or being charged for, report to the Medical Superintendent or District Health Officer. Jan Aushadhi Kendras provide medicines at subsidised rates.

Q: How do I get an Ayushman Bharat health card?
A: Visit any empanelled hospital, Common Service Centre (CSC), or go to pmjay.gov.in with your Aadhaar card. Once eligibility is confirmed, an Ayushman card (Golden Card) is issued free. You can also download a digital card via the Ayushman Bharat app. See file 097b for full details.

Q: There are so many government health schemes — how do I find the right one for my situation?
A: Use this index file to identify the relevant category: insurance/financial help (097b), pregnancy/childbirth/child health (097c), disease testing/treatment/vaccination/mental health (097d), or sanitation/cooking fuel/elderly/disability support (097e). You can also use the myScheme portal (myscheme.gov.in) or the india.gov.in Health & Wellness scheme finder, which lists all central and state schemes and can help check your eligibility for specific schemes.
