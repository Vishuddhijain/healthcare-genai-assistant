--- OVERVIEW ---
The Government of India runs several flagship health programmes to provide accessible and affordable healthcare to all citizens, especially the economically vulnerable. These schemes cover everything from free hospitalisation to maternal care, child immunisation, and mental health services.

--- 1. AYUSHMAN BHARAT - PMJAY ---
Full Name: Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY)
What it is: World's largest government-funded health insurance scheme.
Coverage: Up to ₹5 lakh per family per year for secondary and tertiary hospitalisation.
Who is eligible: Families identified as vulnerable in the Socio-Economic Caste Census (SECC) 2011 — approximately 10.74 crore poor and vulnerable families (50 crore+ individuals).
Services covered: Over 1,500 medical and surgical procedures including surgery, cancer care, kidney dialysis, heart operations.
How to check eligibility: Visit pmjay.gov.in or call 14555.
Empanelled hospitals: Both government and private hospitals are empanelled. Cashless treatment available.

--- 2. AYUSHMAN BHARAT - HEALTH AND WELLNESS CENTRES (HWCs) ---
What it is: 1.5 lakh+ Health and Wellness Centres (HWCs) across India providing comprehensive primary health care.
Services offered FREE:
- General outpatient services
- Maternal and child health services
- Screening for hypertension, diabetes, and 3 common cancers (oral, breast, cervical)
- Basic diagnostic tests
- Essential medicines
- Telehealth consultations
How to find: nhp.gov.in or ask at your local PHC (Primary Health Centre)

--- 3. JANANI SURAKSHA YOJANA (JSY) ---
What it is: Safe motherhood scheme under NHM to reduce maternal and infant mortality.
Who it benefits: Pregnant women, especially from BPL (Below Poverty Line) families in low-performing states.
Benefits:
- Cash assistance to encourage institutional delivery (delivery in hospitals)
- Rural BPL women: ₹1,400; Urban BPL women: ₹1,000
- Free antenatal and postnatal check-ups

--- 4. PRADHAN MANTRI MATRU VANDANA YOJANA (PMMVY) ---
What it is: Maternity benefit programme.
Benefit: ₹5,000 direct bank transfer in three instalments for the first living child.
Eligibility: All pregnant women and lactating mothers.
How to apply: Through ASHA worker, Anganwadi centre, or at government hospital.

--- 5. NATIONAL IMMUNISATION PROGRAMME ---
What it is: Free vaccines for children under India's Universal Immunisation Programme (UIP).
Vaccines provided free:
- BCG (Tuberculosis), Hepatitis B, OPV (Polio), DPT, Hib (Meningitis)
- Measles-Rubella (MR), IPV (Inactivated Polio), Rotavirus, PCV (Pneumonia)
- JE (Japanese Encephalitis) in selected districts
- Td (for adolescents and pregnant women)
Vaccination schedule follows the national immunization calendar from birth to 16 years.
Where: All government hospitals, PHCs, and Anganwadi centres.

--- 6. RASHTRIYA KISHOR SWASTHYA KARYAKRAM (RKSK) ---
What it is: Adolescent health programme (ages 10–19).
Covers: Nutrition, mental health, sexual and reproductive health, substance abuse, injuries, and non-communicable diseases.
Services: Adolescent-friendly health clinics (Saathiya centres) at PHCs.

--- 7. NATIONAL MENTAL HEALTH PROGRAMME (NMHP) ---
What it is: Programme to ensure availability of mental healthcare services at all levels.
District Mental Health Programme (DMHP): Free psychiatric services at district hospitals.
Tele MANAS (launched 2022): National tele mental health helpline.
Helpline: 14416 (24/7 free mental health support)
Also available: iCall helpline: 9152987821 (TISS)

--- 8. MISSION INDRADHANUSH ---
What it is: Intensified immunization programme to reach unvaccinated and partially vaccinated children.
Target: Achieve 90%+ full immunization coverage.
Focus areas: Children aged 0–2 years and pregnant women in low-coverage areas.

--- 9. NATIONAL TOBACCO CONTROL PROGRAMME (NTCP) ---
Provides: Tobacco cessation counselling, awareness campaigns, and support for quitting.
Tobacco quitline: 1800-11-2356 (free)
COTPA (Cigarettes and Other Tobacco Products Act) bans smoking in public places.
