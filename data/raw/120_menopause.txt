--- OVERVIEW ---
Menopause is the permanent cessation of menstruation, marking the end of a woman's reproductive years, typically occurring between ages 45–55 (average age ~51 globally; slightly earlier in India, around 46–48). It is diagnosed retrospectively after 12 consecutive months without a menstrual period, due to natural decline in ovarian function. Perimenopause (the transition period) can last several years and is when most symptoms occur. Menopause is a natural biological process, not a disease, but its symptoms and long-term health implications (bone and cardiovascular health) warrant awareness and management.

--- TYPES ---
1. Natural menopause: Gradual, age-related decline in ovarian function
2. Premature menopause: Before age 40 (premature ovarian insufficiency)
3. Early menopause: Between ages 40–45
4. Induced menopause: Due to surgery (removal of ovaries), chemotherapy, or radiation
5. Perimenopause: Transition phase before menopause with irregular cycles and symptom onset

--- SYMPTOMS ---
Vasomotor symptoms:
- Hot flushes (sudden feeling of heat, often with sweating and flushing of face/chest/neck)
- Night sweats (disrupting sleep)

Menstrual changes (perimenopause):
- Irregular periods (shorter/longer cycles, heavier/lighter flow)

Genitourinary symptoms:
- Vaginal dryness, itching, discomfort during intercourse
- Increased urinary frequency/urgency, recurrent UTIs

Psychological/cognitive:
- Mood changes, irritability, anxiety, low mood
- Sleep disturbances
- Difficulty concentrating, "brain fog"

Physical changes:
- Joint aches and pains
- Weight gain (especially abdominal)
- Hair thinning, dry skin
- Reduced libido

--- WARNING SIGNS ---
- Bleeding after 12 months of no periods (postmenopausal bleeding) — ALWAYS requires medical evaluation to rule out endometrial cancer or other pathology
- Very heavy bleeding during perimenopause — needs evaluation
- Severe mood changes/depression — needs mental health support

--- RISK FACTORS (for early/premature menopause and severe symptoms) ---
- Smoking (associated with earlier menopause)
- Family history of early menopause
- Autoimmune conditions
- Chemotherapy or pelvic radiation
- Surgical removal of ovaries (bilateral oophorectomy)
- Certain genetic conditions (e.g. Turner syndrome, Fragile X)

--- LONG-TERM HEALTH CONSIDERATIONS POST-MENOPAUSE ---
- Osteoporosis: Oestrogen decline accelerates bone loss — increased fracture risk (see Bone Health)
- Cardiovascular disease: Risk increases post-menopause; oestrogen had protective effect
- Increased abdominal fat distribution: Higher metabolic syndrome risk

--- PREVENTION / MANAGEMENT (Lifestyle) ---
- Regular weight-bearing exercise: Maintains bone density and cardiovascular health
- Adequate calcium and Vitamin D intake: Bone health
- Balanced diet: Manage weight, reduce processed foods, adequate protein
- Avoid smoking and limit alcohol
- Manage stress: Yoga, meditation, mindfulness practices
- Sleep hygiene: Cool bedroom, breathable clothing for night sweats
- Pelvic floor exercises (Kegels): For urinary symptoms

--- ENVIRONMENTAL CONTROL ---
- Not applicable in vector/environmental sense — workplace accommodations (temperature control, flexible arrangements) can help manage symptoms like hot flushes

--- VACCINATION ---
- Not directly related; but routine adult vaccinations (influenza, pneumococcal, herpes zoster after 60) become relevant in this age group — see Adult Vaccination topic

--- DIAGNOSIS ---
- Clinical diagnosis: 12 months of amenorrhoea in appropriate age group — usually no tests needed
- FSH (Follicle Stimulating Hormone): Elevated levels support diagnosis in ambiguous cases (e.g. premature menopause evaluation)
- Bone density (DEXA scan): Recommended around menopause for osteoporosis risk assessment
- Lipid profile, blood pressure, blood sugar: Cardiovascular risk assessment

--- TREATMENT (Awareness Only) ---
- Menopausal Hormone Therapy (MHT/HRT): Effective for vasomotor symptoms and genitourinary symptoms; risk-benefit must be individually assessed by a doctor (consideration of cardiovascular, breast cancer, and clot risk factors)
- Non-hormonal options: Certain antidepressants (SSRIs/SNRIs) can reduce hot flushes; vaginal moisturisers/lubricants for dryness; local vaginal oestrogen (low systemic absorption) for genitourinary symptoms
- Calcium and Vitamin D supplementation: For bone health (doctor guided)
- Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT): Effective for mood symptoms and sleep disturbance
- All hormone therapy decisions require individualised medical assessment — not a one-size-fits-all approach

--- WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR ---
- Any bleeding after 12 months without periods (postmenopausal bleeding) — always evaluate
- Symptoms significantly affecting quality of life (severe hot flushes, mood disturbance, sleep problems)
- Menopause before age 40 (premature) — needs evaluation for underlying causes and bone/cardiovascular protection planning
- Bone density assessment around menopause, especially with risk factors

--- INDIA-SPECIFIC CONTEXT ---
- Menopause occurs slightly earlier in Indian women (average ~46–48 years) compared to global average
- Cultural stigma and lack of open discussion about menopause symptoms remain barriers to seeking care
- Osteoporosis risk is compounded by widespread Vitamin D deficiency in India — bone health awareness important post-menopause
- Government health facilities can provide gynaecological evaluation for postmenopausal bleeding and bone health screening (where DEXA available)
- For gynaecological consultation at government hospitals, call 104. For mental health support during menopause, contact Tele MANAS: 14416 or iCall: 9152987821.

--- IMPORTANT SAFETY NOTE ---
This information is for health awareness only. Menopause is a natural transition, not a disease, but symptoms are manageable and long-term health risks (bone, heart) are important to address. Any bleeding after 12 months without periods must be evaluated by a doctor. Consult a qualified healthcare provider for personalised management.

--- FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS ---
Q: What are the most common symptoms of menopause?
A: Hot flushes (sudden heat sensation in upper body), night sweats, irregular periods (before they stop), vaginal dryness, sleep disturbance, mood changes (irritability, anxiety, depression), difficulty concentrating, and weight changes. Symptoms vary widely — some women have minimal impact, others have significant disruption.

Q: At what age does menopause occur in Indian women?
A: The average age of natural menopause in Indian women is 46-47 years — about 4-5 years earlier than Western women. Premature menopause (before 40) occurs in about 1% of women and should be evaluated by a gynaecologist.

Q: Is hormone replacement therapy (HRT) safe?
A: HRT is effective for managing severe menopausal symptoms. Safety depends on individual risk factors (age, cardiovascular health, cancer history). A gynaecologist will assess suitability. HRT is not suitable for everyone — do not start without medical evaluation.

Q: What can I do to manage menopause symptoms naturally?
A: Regular exercise (reduces hot flushes and mood changes), maintaining healthy weight, limiting alcohol and caffeine (worsen hot flushes), yoga and mindfulness (for mood and sleep), dietary calcium and Vitamin D for bone health, and vaginal lubricants for dryness. Discuss all options with a gynaecologist.
