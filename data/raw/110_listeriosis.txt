TOPIC: Listeriosis
CATEGORY: Communicable Disease / Food-Borne
SOURCE: WHO Fact Sheet - https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/listeriosis
LAST UPDATED: 2024

--- OVERVIEW ---
Listeriosis is a foodborne illness caused by Listeria monocytogenes bacteria, found in soil, water, and animals. It can grow at refrigeration temperatures, making it a particular concern for ready-to-eat refrigerated foods. While most healthy people experience mild or no symptoms, listeriosis can be severe and life-threatening in pregnant women (causing miscarriage, stillbirth, preterm birth), newborns, elderly, and immunocompromised individuals. Case fatality in invasive disease can reach 20–30%.

--- TYPES ---
1. Non-invasive (Febrile Gastroenteritis): Mild illness in healthy people — fever, diarrhoea, vomiting
2. Invasive Listeriosis: Bacteria spread to bloodstream/CNS — sepsis, meningitis, encephalitis
3. Pregnancy-associated Listeriosis: Maternal flu-like illness; can cause fetal infection, miscarriage, stillbirth, neonatal sepsis/meningitis

--- SYMPTOMS ---
Mild (healthy individuals): Fever, muscle aches, nausea, diarrhoea (1–3 days incubation)

Invasive disease: Fever, severe headache, stiff neck, confusion, loss of balance, convulsions (meningitis/encephalitis); incubation can be up to 70 days

Pregnancy: Flu-like illness in mother (often mild); can lead to fetal loss or severe neonatal infection

--- WARNING SIGNS ---
- Fever with stiff neck, headache, confusion — possible meningitis (emergency)
- Fever in pregnancy with flu-like symptoms — needs evaluation given fetal risk
- Fever in newborn with poor feeding, lethargy — emergency

--- RISK FACTORS ---
- Pregnant women (~20x more likely to get listeriosis than general population)
- Elderly (>65 years)
- Immunocompromised (cancer, transplant, HIV, diabetes, liver/kidney disease, steroid use)
- Newborns (acquired from infected mother)

--- PREVENTION ---
- Avoid unpasteurised milk and dairy products (soft cheeses made from unpasteurised milk)
- Thoroughly cook raw meat, poultry, seafood
- Wash raw vegetables and fruits thoroughly
- Avoid ready-to-eat refrigerated foods (deli meats, pre-cut fruits) for high-risk groups unless reheated to steaming hot
- Separate raw and cooked foods; clean refrigerators regularly
- Pregnant women: Avoid unpasteurised dairy, raw sprouts, pre-packaged salads unless thoroughly washed

--- ENVIRONMENTAL CONTROL ---
- Food industry: Environmental monitoring for Listeria in processing facilities (especially dairy, meat processing)
- Proper refrigeration temperature maintenance (below 4°C, though Listeria can still grow slowly)

--- VACCINATION ---
- No vaccine available

--- DIAGNOSIS ---
- Blood culture or CSF culture (for meningitis)
- Identification of Listeria monocytogenes from sterile sites

--- TREATMENT (Awareness Only) ---
- Antibiotics: Ampicillin (often with gentamicin) — doctor prescribed; IV treatment for invasive disease
- Hospitalisation required for invasive listeriosis, pregnancy-associated cases, and neonatal infection
- Early treatment in pregnancy may prevent fetal infection

--- WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR ---
- Fever during pregnancy, especially with flu-like symptoms — seek evaluation
- Fever with stiff neck, severe headache, confusion — emergency
- Fever in newborn — emergency
- Elderly or immunocompromised with unexplained fever and gastrointestinal symptoms

--- INDIA-SPECIFIC CONTEXT ---
- Underreported in India due to limited surveillance and testing
- Pregnant women should be counselled on food safety regarding unpasteurised dairy and street food hygiene
- FSSAI food safety guidelines apply to commercial food processing
- For food safety complaints, contact FSSAI: 1800-11-2100. National Health Helpline: 104. Emergency: 112.

--- IMPORTANT SAFETY NOTE ---
This information is for health awareness only. Pregnant women, elderly, and immunocompromised individuals should take extra precautions with food safety. Fever during pregnancy should always be evaluated by a doctor.

--- TREATMENT (AWARENESS ONLY) ---
- Treatment details are for awareness only. Consult a qualified healthcare professional.

--- FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS ---
Q: How do people get listeriosis?
A: Listeriosis is caused by eating food contaminated with Listeria monocytogenes bacteria. Common sources: unpasteurised milk and cheese, ready-to-eat deli meats, smoked fish, pre-packaged salads, and sprouts. Unlike most food bacteria, Listeria grows even in the refrigerator.

Q: Who is at highest risk of severe listeriosis?
A: Pregnant women (can cause miscarriage, stillbirth, or neonatal illness), newborns, elderly, and immunocompromised people (HIV, cancer, transplant patients) are at highest risk. Healthy adults may experience only mild flu-like symptoms.

Q: How can pregnant women prevent listeriosis?
A: Avoid unpasteurised milk and soft cheeses (brie, camembert, feta). Reheat all ready-to-eat meat (deli meats, sausages) to steaming before eating. Avoid raw sprouts. Wash all fruits and vegetables thoroughly. Keep refrigerator below 4°C.

Q: Is listeriosis treatable?
A: Yes. Listeriosis is treated with antibiotics (ampicillin) prescribed by a doctor. Prompt treatment during pregnancy can prevent severe outcomes for the baby. If a pregnant woman has fever and flu-like symptoms and has eaten potentially contaminated food, see a doctor immediately.
