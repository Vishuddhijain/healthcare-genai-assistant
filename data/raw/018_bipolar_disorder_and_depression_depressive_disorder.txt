--- SECTION 1: DEPRESSIVE DISORDER (DEPRESSION) ---

--- OVERVIEW ---
Depression is a common and serious medical condition that negatively affects how you feel, think, and act. It is different from normal sadness or grief. Depression causes persistent feelings of sadness and loss of interest in activities once enjoyed. It is among the leading causes of disability worldwide and is a major contributor to suicide. Depression is highly treatable.

--- KEY FACTS ---
- An estimated 280 million people globally have depression.
- In India, approximately 56 million people suffer from depression.
- Depression is the leading cause of disability worldwide.
- About 700,000 people die by suicide each year globally — depression is a major risk factor.
- Less than 20% of people with depression in India receive any treatment.
- Depression can affect anyone — any age, gender, background.

--- SYMPTOMS ---
Emotional:
- Persistent sad, anxious, or empty mood
- Feelings of hopelessness or worthlessness
- Excessive guilt
- Loss of interest or pleasure in hobbies and activities (anhedonia)
- Feeling helpless
- Thoughts of death or suicide

Physical:
- Fatigue and decreased energy
- Difficulty sleeping or oversleeping
- Changes in appetite (weight loss or gain)
- Unexplained physical pains (headaches, digestive problems)
- Slowed movements and speech
- Difficulty concentrating or making decisions

Diagnosis requires symptoms present for at least 2 weeks.

--- WHAT DEPRESSION IS NOT ---
- NOT a sign of weakness or personal failure
- NOT something you can "just snap out of"
- NOT the same as normal sadness (grief, disappointment)
- NOT caused by laziness
- It is a medical condition with biological, psychological, and social causes

--- CAUSES AND RISK FACTORS ---
- Biological: Genetic predisposition, brain chemistry, hormonal changes (postpartum depression, hypothyroidism)
- Psychological: History of trauma, abuse, loss, low self-esteem
- Social: Social isolation, relationship problems, academic or work stress, poverty
- Medical: Chronic illness, chronic pain, cancer, stroke
- Substance use: Alcohol and drug use can cause or worsen depression

--- TREATMENT ---
Depression is treatable — most people improve with treatment:
- Psychological therapy: Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT), interpersonal therapy
- Medications (antidepressants): Prescribed by a psychiatrist. Take regularly. Do not stop without doctor's advice.
- Lifestyle: Regular exercise (proven to help), consistent sleep, social connection, avoiding alcohol
- Combination of therapy and medication is most effective for moderate-severe depression

India resources:
- Tele MANAS: 14416 (24/7 free support)
- iCall (TISS): 9152987821
- District Hospital Psychiatric OPD: Free consultation
- Vandrevala Foundation: 1860-2662-345

CRISIS: If someone is thinking about suicide or self-harm — call 14416 or 112 immediately.

--- SECTION 2: BIPOLAR DISORDER ---

--- OVERVIEW ---
Bipolar disorder is a mental health condition characterised by extreme mood swings — episodes of mania (abnormally high mood/energy) and depression. Between episodes, the person may feel completely normal. Bipolar disorder is a lifelong condition but is manageable with treatment.

--- KEY FACTS ---
- Bipolar disorder affects approximately 40 million people globally.
- It typically develops in late adolescence or early adulthood (15–25 years).
- Bipolar disorder has one of the highest rates of disability among mental health conditions.
- With proper treatment, most people with bipolar disorder can live normal, productive lives.
- Suicide risk is significantly elevated in untreated bipolar disorder.

--- TYPES ---
1. Bipolar I: Manic episodes lasting at least 7 days (or requiring hospitalisation), plus depressive episodes.
2. Bipolar II: Less severe hypomanic (not full mania) episodes plus depressive episodes.
3. Cyclothymia: Milder cycling of moods for 2+ years.

--- SYMPTOMS ---
Manic Episode:
- Abnormally elevated or irritable mood
- Inflated self-esteem or grandiosity
- Decreased need for sleep (feels rested after 3 hours)
- More talkative than usual; racing thoughts
- Easily distracted
- Increased goal-directed activity or physical agitation
- Risky behaviour: reckless spending, sexual impulsivity, poor business decisions
- Severe: may include psychosis (delusions, hallucinations)

Depressive Episode:
- Same as depression symptoms listed above

Triggers for episodes:
- Disrupted sleep
- Substance use
- Stressful life events
- Not taking medication regularly

--- TREATMENT ---
Bipolar disorder requires lifelong treatment:
- Mood stabilisers (lithium, valproate) — mainstay of treatment, prescribed by psychiatrist
- Antipsychotic medications — for manic episodes
- Antidepressants — used carefully (can trigger mania)
- Psychotherapy: CBT, family-focused therapy
- Lifestyle: Regular sleep schedule, avoiding alcohol/drugs, stress management

KEY: Never stop medications without doctor's advice — even when feeling well. Stopping causes relapse.

--- WHEN TO SEEK HELP ---
- Symptoms of mania or depression that last days and affect daily functioning
- Thoughts of suicide or self-harm
- Family member showing unusual, extreme behavioural changes
- First episode of mania often requires hospitalisation for safety

--- FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS ---
Q: Does depression mean I'm weak?
A: Absolutely not. Depression is a medical illness — like diabetes or hypertension. It involves changes in brain chemistry. Seeking help for depression takes courage, not weakness.

Q: Can antidepressants be stopped once I feel better?
A: No — not without your doctor's guidance. Stopping suddenly can cause withdrawal and relapse. Doctors usually taper the dose gradually. Always follow medical advice.

Q: Is bipolar disorder the same as mood swings?
A: Normal mood swings last hours. Bipolar episodes of mania or depression last days to weeks and severely impact daily functioning. If in doubt, consult a psychiatrist.

Q: Can bipolar disorder be cured?
A: Not cured, but it is very manageable. Most people with proper treatment (medication + therapy + lifestyle) maintain stable moods and live full, productive lives.

--- IMPORTANT SAFETY NOTE ---
Depression and bipolar disorder are serious medical conditions. Seek professional help. In a crisis or suicidal emergency, call 112 or Tele MANAS: 14416 immediately.
