--- OVERVIEW ---
Women's health encompasses a broad range of physical and mental health topics. Maternal health — the health of women during pregnancy, childbirth, and the postnatal period — is a key public health priority. India has made significant progress in reducing maternal and infant mortality but challenges remain, particularly in rural areas.

--- ANTENATAL CARE (DURING PREGNANCY) ---
All pregnant women should have at least 4 antenatal check-ups (ANC visits):
- 1st visit: Before 12 weeks (first trimester)
- 2nd visit: Between 14–26 weeks
- 3rd visit: Between 28–34 weeks
- 4th visit: Between 36 weeks and delivery

At each ANC visit:
- Blood pressure check
- Weight and height measurement (BMI)
- Haemoglobin (anaemia check)
- Blood sugar screening
- Urine test
- Ultrasound as recommended
- Tetanus Toxoid (TT) injection (2 doses)
- Iron and folic acid supplements (free at government health centres)

Free ANC services: Available at all government PHCs, CHCs, and hospitals under Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK).

--- DANGER SIGNS DURING PREGNANCY ---
Seek medical care IMMEDIATELY if you have:
- Heavy vaginal bleeding at any time
- Severe headache, blurred vision, or swelling of hands/face (signs of preeclampsia)
- High fever
- Severe abdominal pain
- Reduced or absent fetal movements after 28 weeks
- Leaking of fluid before 37 weeks (early labour)

--- SAFE DELIVERY ---
- All deliveries should ideally occur in a health facility with trained birth attendants
- Free institutional delivery at government hospitals under Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY)
- Cash incentive of ₹1,400 for rural and ₹1,000 for urban BPL women for institutional delivery
- Trained Dais (birth attendants) available in remote areas through NHM

--- POSTNATAL CARE ---
After delivery:
- At least 3 postnatal check-ups in the first 6 weeks
- Watch for danger signs: heavy bleeding, fever, breast infection, signs of depression
- Breastfeeding: Initiate within 1 hour of birth. Exclusive breastfeeding for the first 6 months.
- Contraception counselling at postnatal visits (family planning choices)

--- BREAST HEALTH AND CANCER AWARENESS ---
- Breast cancer is the most common cancer in Indian women (25–31% of all cancers in women)
- Average age of onset is shifting from 50–70 to 30–50 years in India
- Perform monthly self-examination — look and feel for any new lumps or changes
- Mammography screening recommended from age 40 onwards (or earlier if family history)
- Free screening under Ayushman Bharat HWCs

Warning signs to report to a doctor:
- New lump in the breast or armpit
- Change in size, shape, or appearance of breast
- Nipple discharge (not breastmilk)
- Skin changes on the breast (redness, dimpling)

--- CERVICAL CANCER AWARENESS ---
- Cervical cancer is the second most common cancer in Indian women
- Caused by persistent infection with Human Papillomavirus (HPV)
- Pap smear screening can detect pre-cancerous changes early — recommended from age 21 every 3 years
- HPV vaccine provides strong protection — recommended for girls aged 9–14 years (before sexual activity)
- Free cervical cancer screening at government Health and Wellness Centres for women aged 30–65

--- MENSTRUAL HEALTH ---
- Regular menstrual cycles (21–35 days) are normal
- See a doctor if: periods are very heavy, very painful (affecting daily life), irregular, or if you miss 3+ periods without pregnancy
- Anaemia: Very common in Indian women due to menstrual blood loss. Eat iron-rich foods. Free iron-folic acid tablets at government health centres.
- Menstrual hygiene: Use clean, preferably disposable or properly washed reusable pads. Change every 4–6 hours. Subsidised sanitary pads under Suvidha scheme at Jan Aushadhi stores.

--- FAMILY PLANNING (AWARENESS) ---
Free family planning services and contraceptives are available at all government health facilities under the National Family Planning Programme. Options include:
- Oral contraceptive pills
- Copper-T (IUD)
- Male and female condoms
- Emergency contraception
- Permanent methods (sterilisation)
A healthcare provider should be consulted to choose the appropriate method.

--- NUTRITION FOR WOMEN ---
- Pregnant and lactating women need extra iron, folic acid, calcium, and protein
- Daily supplements: Iron-Folic Acid (IFA) tablets are provided free at government health centres
- Eat iron-rich foods: green leafy vegetables, pulses, jaggery, dates, meat
- Vitamin C (lemon, amla) helps iron absorption
- Avoid raw papaya, raw eggs, undercooked meat during pregnancy

--- IMPORTANT SAFETY NOTE ---
This is awareness information only. All pregnancy, delivery, and post-delivery care must be provided by or under the supervision of qualified healthcare professionals. Never attempt home delivery without trained assistance. For emergencies call 112.
