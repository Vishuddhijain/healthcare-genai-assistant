TOPIC: Leprosy (Hansen's Disease)
CATEGORY: Communicable Disease / Neglected Tropical Disease
SOURCE: WHO Fact Sheet - https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/leprosy
LAST UPDATED: 2024

--- OVERVIEW ---
Leprosy is a chronic infectious disease caused by Mycobacterium leprae, primarily affecting skin and peripheral nerves. India declared national elimination (<1/10,000) in 2005 but still accounts for ~50–60% of global new cases (~75,000–100,000 annually). It is curable with free Multi-Drug Therapy (MDT).

--- TYPES ---
1. Paucibacillary (PB) leprosy: Few lesions, less contagious, 6-month MDT
2. Multibacillary (MB) leprosy: Many lesions, high bacterial load, more contagious, 12-month MDT

--- SYMPTOMS ---
- Hypopigmented or reddish skin patches with reduced/absent sensation (key feature)
- Thickened, painful peripheral nerves (ulnar, peroneal, facial nerves)
- Muscle weakness: foot drop, claw hand, lagophthalmos (eye won't close)
- Nasal congestion, nosebleeds (MB leprosy)
- Leonine facies (severe lepromatous cases)

--- WARNING SIGNS ---
- Sudden inflammation of skin lesions/nerves (Type 1/Reversal reaction) — needs urgent steroids to prevent permanent nerve damage
- Painful nodules with fever (Type 2/ENL reaction) — needs urgent treatment
- New numbness or weakness in hands/feet/face — urgent evaluation to prevent disability

--- RISK FACTORS ---
- Close, prolonged contact with untreated multibacillary leprosy patient
- Household contacts of leprosy patients
- Living in endemic areas with limited healthcare access

--- PREVENTION ---
- BCG vaccination provides partial protection (50–80%)
- Post-exposure prophylaxis: Single-dose rifampicin (SDR) for close contacts under NLEP
- Early diagnosis and treatment eliminates infectivity within days

--- ENVIRONMENTAL CONTROL ---
- Not vector-borne; no environmental vector control needed
- Active case finding campaigns in endemic districts under NLEP

--- VACCINATION ---
- BCG vaccine (given at birth under UIP) provides partial cross-protection against leprosy

--- DIAGNOSIS ---
- Clinical: Hypopigmented anaesthetic patch + thickened nerve
- Skin slit smear for AFB (multibacillary cases)
- Skin biopsy for histopathology confirmation

--- TREATMENT (Awareness Only) ---
- Multi-Drug Therapy (MDT): Rifampicin + Dapsone (± Clofazimine for MB) — FREE at all government facilities
- PB: 6 months; MB: 12 months
- Reactions (Type 1/2): Corticosteroids, thalidomide (ENL) — doctor prescribed
- Disability prevention: Proper footwear, eye care, physiotherapy

--- WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR ---
- Any numb skin patch — even a single one — should be evaluated
- Hand weakness (claw hand), foot drop, or eye not closing fully
- Sudden severe reaction in existing leprosy patient — emergency
- Household contact of leprosy patient — for SDR prophylaxis

--- INDIA-SPECIFIC CONTEXT ---
- National Leprosy Eradication Programme (NLEP): Free MDT at all government health facilities
- Leprosy is curable — discrimination is being addressed through legal reforms (removal of discriminatory provisions from various Acts)
- District leprosy offices coordinate case detection and treatment

--- IMPORTANT SAFETY NOTE ---
This information is for health awareness only. Leprosy is curable and treatment is free at government facilities. Any numb skin patch should be evaluated promptly — early treatment prevents disability. There is no justification for stigma or discrimination.

--- TREATMENT (AWARENESS ONLY) ---
- Treatment details are for awareness only. Consult a qualified healthcare professional.

--- FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS ---
Q: Is leprosy still a problem in India?
A: Yes. India accounts for about 60% of all new global leprosy cases. While India achieved official elimination (less than 1 case per 10,000) nationally in 2005, some states and districts still have high case loads. Free detection and treatment under the National Leprosy Eradication Programme (NLEP).

Q: Is leprosy highly contagious?
A: No. Leprosy is one of the least contagious infectious diseases. About 95% of people have natural immunity. Prolonged close contact with an untreated patient is required for transmission. Casual contact — handshaking, sitting together, sharing meals — does NOT transmit leprosy.

Q: What are the early signs of leprosy?
A: The earliest sign is a pale or reddish skin patch with reduced or absent sensation (cannot feel touch, pain, or temperature). Other signs: thickened nerves (felt as lumps), muscle weakness of hands or feet, and non-healing skin wounds. See a doctor at any PHC for free evaluation.

Q: Can leprosy be cured?
A: Yes. Leprosy is fully curable with Multi-Drug Therapy (MDT), which is provided FREE at all government PHCs and hospitals under NLEP. Paucibacillary leprosy: 6 months treatment. Multibacillary leprosy: 12 months treatment. Early treatment prevents disability.
