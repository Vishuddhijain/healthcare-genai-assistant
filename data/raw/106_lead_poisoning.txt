TOPIC: Lead Poisoning
CATEGORY: Environmental Health / Toxicology
SOURCE: WHO Fact Sheet - https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/lead-poisoning-and-health
LAST UPDATED: 2024

--- OVERVIEW ---
Lead is a toxic metal with no safe level of exposure, especially for children, causing irreversible neurodevelopmental damage. WHO estimates lead exposure causes ~21.7 million DALYs lost globally. In India, ICMR studies show widespread elevated blood lead levels in children, linked to battery recycling, leaded paint, and spice adulteration.

--- TYPES ---
- Acute lead poisoning: High-dose exposure causing encephalopathy (rare, emergency)
- Chronic lead poisoning: Low-level ongoing exposure causing cumulative neurological and organ damage (most common form)

--- SYMPTOMS ---
Children:
- Reduced IQ, learning and memory problems
- Behavioural issues (hyperactivity, aggression)
- Delayed development, hearing loss, anaemia, reduced growth
- Abdominal pain, constipation, loss of appetite

Adults:
- Hypertension, kidney damage, peripheral neuropathy (wrist drop)
- Reduced fertility, miscarriage risk
- Cognitive decline (occupational exposure)

--- WARNING SIGNS ---
- Acute encephalopathy: Severe abdominal pain (lead colic), seizures, delirium, coma — MEDICAL EMERGENCY
- Unexplained learning/behavioural issues in child living near industrial sites

--- RISK FACTORS ---
- Informal lead-acid battery recycling (major India source)
- Lead-based paint in older buildings (India banned >90ppm in 2016)
- Adulterated spices (turmeric, chilli with lead chromate)
- Kohl/surma/sindoor cosmetics containing lead
- Occupational exposure (battery, smelting, painting, plumbing)
- Imported toys/jewellery with high lead content

--- PREVENTION ---
- Avoid kohl/surma/sindoor near children and pregnant women
- Buy spices from reputable, tested sources
- Damp-mop floors (not dry sweep) in older painted homes
- Wash children's hands frequently, especially before meals
- Avoid unverified imported toys for young children
- Adequate dietary calcium, iron, Vitamin C reduces lead absorption

--- ENVIRONMENTAL CONTROL ---
- Enforce Lead Paint Rules 2016 (90 ppm limit)
- Relocate informal battery recycling away from residential areas
- Worker PPE (respirators, gloves) and no eating at worksite
- FSSAI spice testing for adulteration

--- VACCINATION ---
- Not applicable; no vaccine for lead poisoning

--- DIAGNOSIS ---
- Blood Lead Level (BLL): >5 µg/dL in children warrants source investigation; >45 µg/dL needs chelation
- Erythrocyte protoporphyrin (screening)
- X-ray long bones: "Lead lines" in chronic high exposure children

--- TREATMENT (Awareness Only) ---
- Remove from exposure source (primary step)
- Nutritional support (calcium, iron, Vitamin C)
- Chelation therapy (DMSA oral, EDTA/Dimercaprol IV for severe cases) — hospital, specialist-only
- Educational support for affected children

--- WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR ---
- Child near battery recycling/old painted building with learning/behaviour problems
- Suspected ingestion of lead-containing paint, cosmetics, or medicines
- Occupational lead workers — periodic blood lead testing
- Abdominal pain/weakness with known lead exposure

--- INDIA-SPECIFIC CONTEXT ---
- ICMR studies show elevated BLLs in children in Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata and industrial belts
- Spice adulteration with lead chromate remains a FSSAI enforcement priority
- Informal battery recycling exposes millions of workers/communities
- Surma/kajal use remains common — public awareness campaigns needed

--- IMPORTANT SAFETY NOTE ---
This information is for health awareness only. There is no safe lead level for children. If exposure is suspected, consult a doctor for blood lead testing and source elimination.

--- TREATMENT (AWARENESS ONLY) ---
- Treatment details are for awareness only. Consult a qualified healthcare professional.
