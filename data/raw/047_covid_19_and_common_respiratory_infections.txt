--- OVERVIEW ---
Respiratory infections range from the common cold and flu (influenza) to more serious illnesses like COVID-19. They primarily affect the nose, throat, windpipe, and lungs. Most mild respiratory infections recover on their own with rest, but some can lead to serious complications especially in vulnerable groups.

--- COVID-19 ---
COVID-19 is caused by the SARS-CoV-2 coronavirus. It was declared a pandemic in March 2020 and remains in circulation as an endemic virus.

How it spreads:
- Primarily through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, talks, or breathes
- Close contact with an infected person
- Touching contaminated surfaces and then touching face (less common)

Symptoms:
- Fever or chills
- Cough (dry or productive)
- Fatigue
- Loss of taste or smell
- Sore throat
- Headache
- Shortness of breath (in moderate to severe cases)
- Body aches

High-risk groups for severe COVID-19:
- Elderly adults (especially 60+)
- People with diabetes, hypertension, heart disease, obesity, or compromised immune systems
- Pregnant women
- People who are unvaccinated

Prevention:
- Stay up to date with COVID-19 vaccinations (CoWIN portal: cowin.gov.in)
- Wash hands frequently with soap for at least 20 seconds
- Use hand sanitiser when soap is unavailable
- Wear a mask in crowded or poorly ventilated areas
- Maintain physical distance in high-risk settings
- Ensure good ventilation in indoor spaces
- Avoid touching eyes, nose, and mouth with unwashed hands

--- INFLUENZA (SEASONAL FLU) ---
Influenza is a common respiratory infection caused by influenza viruses.

Symptoms (similar to COVID but typically more sudden):
- Sudden high fever
- Body aches and muscle pain
- Cough and sore throat
- Runny or stuffy nose
- Headache
- Fatigue
- Vomiting/diarrhoea (more common in children)

Prevention:
- Annual flu vaccine (especially recommended for elderly, children, pregnant women, and healthcare workers)
- Good respiratory hygiene: cover mouth with elbow when coughing or sneezing
- Frequent handwashing
- Stay home when sick to prevent spread

--- COMMON COLD ---
Caused by various viruses including rhinovirus. Very common, usually mild.
Symptoms: Runny nose, sneezing, sore throat, mild cough, low-grade fever.
Recovery: Usually within 7–10 days without any specific treatment.
Management: Rest, fluids, steam inhalation, and OTC decongestants (consult pharmacist).

--- GENERAL RESPIRATORY HYGIENE ---
1. Cover your mouth and nose with a bent elbow or tissue when you cough or sneeze.
2. Dispose of used tissues immediately.
3. Wash your hands often.
4. Avoid touching your face.
5. Stay home if you are sick.
6. Keep indoor spaces well-ventilated.

--- WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR ---
Seek medical attention if you have:
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Persistent chest pain or pressure
- Confusion or difficulty staying awake
- Bluish lips or face
- Fever above 104°F (40°C) that does not come down
- Worsening symptoms after initially feeling better
- Symptoms in infants, elderly, pregnant women, or people with chronic diseases

EMERGENCY: Call 112 for severe breathing difficulty.

--- VACCINATION (INDIA) ---
COVID-19 vaccines are available free at government health centres.
Check your nearest vaccination centre: cowin.gov.in or call 1075 (health helpline).

--- IMPORTANT SAFETY NOTE ---
Do not take antibiotics for viral infections like cold, flu, or COVID-19 — they are ineffective and increase antibiotic resistance. Always consult a doctor for appropriate treatment.

--- FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS ---
Q: Is COVID-19 still a threat in 2024?
A: COVID-19 continues to circulate as an endemic virus with periodic surges from new variants. While most current infections are milder than earlier in the pandemic, high-risk groups (elderly, immunocompromised, unvaccinated) remain vulnerable to severe disease. Staying up to date with vaccines remains important.

Q: Do I need a COVID-19 booster dose in India?
A: Booster recommendations change based on current guidelines. Check with your nearest government hospital or cowin.gov.in for current booster recommendations for your age group and risk status. High-risk groups (elderly, immunocompromised) are generally prioritised for boosters.

Q: What is the difference between a cold, flu, and COVID-19?

Q: Should I still wear a mask in public in India?
A: Mask wearing is no longer mandatory in most public settings in India but remains recommended in crowded indoor spaces, hospitals, and for high-risk individuals. During surges or if symptomatic, masking helps protect others. N95 masks provide the best protection.
