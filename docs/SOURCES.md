## Topics Covered (235 files)

The raw knowledge-base files are numbered in alphabetical order by topic title. File 097 (Indian Government Health Schemes and Public Resources) was expanded from a single generic overview into a focused overview plus four detailed sub-files (097b–097e) covering health insurance/financial protection, maternal/child/adolescent health, disease control/immunisation/primary care, and sanitation/nutrition/elderly/disability welfare schemes — reflecting the breadth of the 300+ schemes listed under the Health & Wellness category on india.gov.in.

## Data Collection Method

1. Source identification: WHO fact sheets were used as the primary source for A-Z public-health topics. Indian sources such as NHP, NHM, MoHFW, NIMHANS, NCDC, ICMR, FSSAI, and programme portals are referenced where already present or relevant.
2. Data extraction: Awareness content was structured into topic-specific text files for retrieval-augmented generation.
3. Structuring: Each file includes labelled sections for overview, key facts, types, symptoms, warning signs, risk factors, prevention, environmental control, vaccination, diagnosis, treatment awareness only, when to see a doctor, and India-specific context where applicable.
4. Safety formatting: Treatment is kept awareness-only; files do not provide prescriptions, dosages, or personal diagnosis.
5. Deduplication: WHO fact sheets already represented inside broader grouped files were skipped instead of duplicated.

## Assignment Scope Mapping

- Common diseases: symptoms and prevention from WHO fact sheets and existing NHP-linked files.
- Preventive care and wellness: diet, exercise, hygiene, nutrition, self-care, and environmental risk reduction.
- Government schemes and health systems: existing NHM/NHA/MoHFW files plus WHO health-financing and UHC awareness where relevant.
- Emergency awareness: warning signs and when-to-see-a-doctor sections across disease, injury, poisoning, and outbreak topics.
- Mental health awareness: WHO mental health topics, with NIMHANS referenced in existing India-specific mental health files.

## Licensing / Usage Rights

WHO pages are publicly accessible health education resources. Existing Indian government and institutional sources are retained for public-health awareness context.

## What Is NOT Covered (By Design — Safety)

- Specific medicine names, dosages, or drug interactions
- Medical diagnosis for any condition
- Treatment protocols
- Surgical instructions

All chatbot responses should redirect personal medical decisions to qualified clinicians.
